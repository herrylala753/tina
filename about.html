About Woldia University

Woldia University is a public university located in Woldia Town, North Wollo Zone, Amhara Region, Ethiopia, established in 2011. It offers undergraduate, postgraduate, and PhD programs across various colleges and faculties, including Health Sciences, Agriculture, and Social Sciences, with two main campuses: the main Woldia Campus and the Mersa campus. The university's mission includes teaching, research, and community service to support Ethiopia's development. Establishment and location.

Woldia Town, the capital of the Semien Wollo Zone in Ethiopia's Amhara region.
Approximately 525 km north of Addis Ababa.

Campuses

Main Campus: Woldia University Campus.
Mersa Campus: Located 30 km from the main campus, this campus is known for its focus on agriculture.
St. Lalibela Institute of Heritage and Tourism Studies: A third campus focusing on heritage and tourism.
